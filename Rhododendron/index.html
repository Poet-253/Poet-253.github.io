Pacific Rhododendron

"The Pacific Rhododendron (Rhododendron macrophyllum), also known as azaleas are common amongst the Western side of the Cascades as well as found in Asian countries like China, Japan, and other neighboring areas of the same climate."
Some key facts to know about the Pacific Rhododendron:
    It reaches to about 24 feet (8m); taller in the shade than in the sun.
    It grows in in fairly dry open forests.
    It is the state flower for Washington State.
